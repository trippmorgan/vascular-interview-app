## üì± HOW TO ADD TEMPLATES TO PLAUD

### Step 1: Open PLAUD App
- Launch the PLAUD app on your phone
- Make sure your PLAUD device is connected

### Step 2: Create a Custom Template
1. Tap **Settings** (gear icon)
2. Tap **Custom Templates**
3. Tap **+ Create New Template**
4. Give it a name (e.g., "PAD Template")

### Step 3: Copy & Paste Template Content
1. Open the appropriate template file from this folder (e.g., `PAD-TEMPLATE.md`)
2. **Copy the entire content** (minus the title line)
3. **Paste into PLAUD's template editor**
4. Save the template

### Step 4: Repeat for Other Templates
- Create a separate custom template for each condition type
- Name them clearly: "Universal Template," "Venous Template," "Carotid Template," etc.

## üî§ HOW TO ADD CUSTOM VOCABULARY

PLAUD has a **Custom Vocabulary** feature that improves transcription accuracy for medical terms.

### Step 1: Open Custom Vocabulary
1. Tap **Settings** (gear icon)
2. Tap **Custom Vocabulary**
3. Tap **+ Add Words**

### Step 2: Add Medical Terms
1. Open **CUSTOM-VOCABULARY.md** from this folder
2. **Copy terms from relevant sections** (procedures, medications, anatomy, etc.)
3. **Paste into PLAUD's custom vocabulary list**
4. Save

**Tip:** You don't need to add every term at once. Start with the most commonly used terms (procedures, medications, anatomical terms), then add more over time.

## ‚öôÔ∏è TIPS FOR BEST RESULTS

### Recording Tips
- **Place PLAUD near the speaker** (on desk, clip to clothing)
- **Speak clearly** ‚Äî enunciate medical terms
- **State key findings explicitly** (e.g., "Right dorsalis pedis pulse is absent")
- **Minimize background noise** if possible

### Template Optimization
- **Edit templates to match your workflow** ‚Äî These are starting points, not rigid rules
- **Add your preferred phrasing** if you have standard questions you always ask
- **Remove sections you don't use** to make output more concise

### Custom Vocabulary
- **Add new terms as you encounter them** ‚Äî Expand the vocabulary list over time
- **Include brand names** of medications you commonly prescribe
- **Add anatomical variants** or terms specific to your practice

## üîß TROUBLESHOOTING

### "PLAUD isn't extracting the information I need"
- **Solution:** Edit your template to be more explicit. Add specific bullet points for what you want captured. Example: Change "Document pulse exam" to "Document each pulse as Present/Absent/Dopplerable: Right femoral, Left femoral, Right DP, Left DP..."

### "Transcription has errors with medical terms"
- **Solution:** Add those terms to Custom Vocabulary. Spell them exactly as they should appear.

### "Summary is too long / too short"
- **Solution:** Adjust your template. Remove unnecessary sections to shorten. Add more detailed subsections to lengthen.

### "PLAUD summary format doesn't match template exactly"
- **Note:** PLAUD's AI interprets the template as guidance, not rigid instructions. It will extract relevant information but may not follow the exact format. This is normal. Review and edit the output.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT NOTES

### HIPAA & Privacy
- **PLAUD recordings contain PHI (Protected Health Information)**
- Follow your institution's policies on recording patient encounters
- Obtain patient consent if required
- Secure your PLAUD device and app with password/biometric lock
- Do not share recordings or transcripts inappropriately

### Clinical Responsibility
- **AI-generated summaries are tools, not replacements for clinical judgment**
- Always **review and edit** the summary before finalizing
- Verify **critical data** (measurements, medications, test results)
- PLAUD may miss or misinterpret information ‚Äî your review is essential

### Limitations
- PLAUD works best in **quiet environments**
- Transcription accuracy decreases with **heavy accents** or **mumbling**
- AI summaries are **not perfect** ‚Äî expect to make edits
- Templates are **guidance** ‚Äî AI interprets them, doesn't follow them rigidly
